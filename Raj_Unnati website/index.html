The Imperative for Transition

Why Raj-UNNATI?

Addressing structural gaps in administration, tracking, and delivery to transform governance from inputs to outcomes.

Administrative Lacuna

Gaps in traditional online monitoring platforms where administrative gridlocks between department silos remain unresolved, delaying public works.

Departmental Silos

Accountability Lacuna

Weak enforcement mechanisms for delayed projects and recurring grievances. Decisions lack central administrative tracking, leading to administrative slippage.

Weak Enforcement

Delivery Quality Lacuna

Significant service delays where compliance is measured purely on financial expenditure (input) rather than tangible public utility (outcomes).

Operational Process Flow

How It Works

The step-by-step administrative timeline for resolving complex public grievances and executing major infrastructure outlays.

1

Issue Identified

Major infrastructure delay or critical public grievance registered.

2

Department Uploads

Concerned department logs current status, blocks, and justifications.

3

CMIS Review

Core team analyzes the blocks and tags dependencies across ministries.

4

Raj-UNNATI Meeting

Joint digital review chaired by the Chief Minister and Chief Secretary.

5

Decision Issued

Immediate executive ruling with strict implementation timelines.

6

Tracking

Weekly progress tracking via CMIS to monitor enforcement metrics.

7

Verification

Ground-level outcomes verified and documented for public reporting.

Accountability Framework

Who is Responsible for What?

Defining roles across the governance ladder to prevent overlapping duties and secure end-to-end outcome metrics.

Chief Minister

Strategic Executive Head
  • Strategic leadership and governance direction
  • Presiding over monthly review conferences
  • Ultimate decision making on inter-departmental conflicts
  • Direct administrative accountability reviews

Chief Secretary

Operational Implementation Head
  • Cross-department compliance oversight
  • Monthly pre-meeting coordination agendas
  • Escalating unresolved bottlenecks to CMIS
  • Administrative reform execution monitoring

Department Secretaries

Policy & Sector Execution Nodes
  • Project implementation and timeline tracking
  • Weekly updates of delays onto the CMIS system
  • Direct oversight of department field agencies
  • Submitting formal compliance audits post-ruling

District Collectors

Ground Execution Nodes
  • Ground implementation and local approvals
  • Resolving land acquisition and forest clearance blocks
  • Field verification of structural completion claims
  • Local grievance resolution tracking

Citizens

Public Stakeholders
  • Registering project performance issues
  • Participating in digital outcome verification
  • Independent feedback on project quality
  • Validating grievance resolution on ground

Strategic Policy Recommendations

RAJ-UNNATI 2.0

Future governance roadmaps aimed at expanding data connectivity, reinforcing compliance audits, and securing citizen verification mechanisms.

R1

SAMANVAY

Cross-Ministry Connectivity within CMIS

Purpose: Breaks bureaucratic silos by creating automated data links between conflicting line departments in the CMIS system.

Expected Outcome: 35% drop in file clearances and multi-department approvals.

R2

PRABANDHAN

Permanent Program Management Authority

Purpose: Sets up an independent, non-partisan governance authority to preserve program continuity and administrative knowledge during transitions.

Expected Outcome: Seamless continuation of long-term outlays irrespective of leadership changes.

R3

JAN-DRISHTI

Citizen Outcome Verification

Purpose: Integrates automated citizen feedback loops to verify field work quality and check false compliance logging.

Expected Outcome: Independent verification of welfare projects, reducing false completion reporting.

R4

UNNATI SCORE

District Governance Ranking System

Purpose: Implements competitive benchmarking metrics across all districts, ranking officers based on delivery targets.

1st: Jaipur (92.4) 2nd: Alwar (88.1) 3rd: Kota (86.5)

Expected Metrics: Service delivery, grievance resolution rates, project completion timelines, and citizen satisfaction scores.

R5

OPEN DATA PORTAL

Public Transparency Portal

Purpose: Opens anonymized administrative performance datasets to researchers and the public to promote civic research.

Expected Outcome: Enhances government research, media accountability, and civic trust index.

PUBLIC POLICY ANALYSIS

About This Research

This high-fidelity prototype website was developed as part of an academic research project analyzing state-level replications of federal monitoring frameworks in India.

Research Topic: Raj-UNNATI: A State-Level Replication of the PRAGATI Governance Model
Researcher: Lakshya Kumawat
Program: B.S. Management and Public Policy

The analysis details how state governments can model internal digital interfaces to establish accountability pathways, synchronize intersecting administrative agencies, and transition operational targets from budget allocations (inputs) to public assets (outcomes).

Core Research Inquiries

A

Federal-to-State Scalability

Evaluating if the centralized PRAGATI system structure can be scaled down to state Secretariats to coordinate districts.

B

Departmental Cohesion

Tracking if digital monitoring platforms help remove conflicting land and environment issues across departments.

C

Outcome Measurement

Shifting metrics from financial expenditures to physical execution and community access validation.
